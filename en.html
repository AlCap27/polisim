MRP Bayesian Dirichlet-Multinomial

Architecture: Dirichlet-Multinomial multi-coalition (PyMC). 252 observations (2018–2025), 54 demographic cells per constituency, r_hat=1.000, 10 divergences over 8,000 samples. Output: posterior median + 90% credibility intervals + P(win) per constituency. RMSE 4.3pp out-of-sample (Lombardia 2023 blind test). Freeze: 29 April 2026.

Multi-step flow · Coherence evaluation · Heuristic override

Step 1 (national simulator — 9 pre-loaded party profiles based on ITANES 2022, PolitPro live polling updated daily) feeds automatically into Step 2 (constituency-level MRP). A second Claude instance evaluates message-territory coherence: 0–1 score, rejection risk classification (low / medium / high / critical), estimated impact in percentage points, explicit alert when coherence falls below threshold. A heuristic override layer lets the operator correct the MRP output with local political knowledge the model cannot have — weak parachuted candidate, strong civic list, local dynamics not captured by demographics. Both outputs (pure MRP and operator-corrected) are shown side by side.

Methodological transparency · Declared limitations

What PoliSim does not yet do

Three active limitations at launch. Declaring them is a design choice, not a weakness.

⚠ Limitation 1 · LLM layer

The message optimisation layer (Stage 3) uses Claude Sonnet. Scores for resonance, credibility, and differentiation are calibrated on expert judgement and ITANES data — not validated on real campaign outcomes. Treat results as hypotheses to test.

⚠ Limitation 2 · Macro-area swing model

The MRP model reasons about aggregate coalitions (CDX/CSX/M5S/Other), not individual parties. Party-level estimates are on the post-validation roadmap. Coherence evaluation scores are exploratory — not validated on real campaign outcomes.

⚠ Limitation 3 · Uncalibrated weights

Psychographic profile weights are calibrated on ITANES 2022 (2022 general election) but not verified on 2024-2026 campaigns. Social listening integration for continuous recalibration is planned for v2.0.
