It is the analytical engine behind Q-Italia, the Italian political movement using data and declared methodology to bridge the complexity gap in democratic communication.

Stage 1 — Electoral Simulation

Seat distribution modelling for Italy's 2027 elections across all uninominal constituencies (Camera and Senato). The model uses a Uniform Partisan Swing (UPS) approach calibrated on official Eligendo OpenData from the Ministry of Interior, weighted by historical volatility and constituency competitiveness.

Critically, the simulation verifies both-chamber majority simultaneously — the real constitutional condition for Italian government formation, which requires a majority in both Camera and Senato independently.

Stage 2 — Demographic Profiling (MRP)

Following the methodology of Wang, Rothschild, Goel & Gelman (2015), PoliSim applies Multilevel Regression with Poststratification to generate reliable local estimates from national survey data. Demographic profiles are anchored to ISTAT 2021 Permanent Census and Labour Force Survey data — not qualitative assumptions.

An ensemble Ridge + Random Forest model (validated by Graefe et al., 2021) captures both linear relationships and non-linear demographic interactions, calibrated across unemployment rates, poverty indices, NEET indicators, and educational attainment per constituency.

Stage 3 — Message Optimisation

Message generation follows Entman's Framing Theory (1993) across four dimensions: problem definition, causal diagnosis, moral evaluation, and solution recommendation. Each topic generates four message variants with distinct framing.

Generation and scoring are performed by Claude Sonnet 4.6 (Anthropic). Scoring metrics are domain-specific: political engagement uses different indicators than NGO donor activation or brand consumer propensity.

Declared methodological limitations (published at launch): (1) The LLM layer has not been empirically validated on real campaign outcomes. (2) Scoring weights are expert-calibrated, not derived from observed behavioural data. (3) The swing model operates at macro-area level — per-constituency shapefile integration is in development. Transparency on limitations is a design principle.

Political Parties & Consultants

Electoral scenario modelling, swing district identification, message optimisation per constituency segment. Metrics: electoral engagement, coalition buildability, undecided voter resonance, abstentionist activation.

NGOs — Fundraising & Legacy Giving

Donor segment profiling, legacy giving communication optimisation, institutional trust calibration. Specific metrics for end-of-life giving sensitivity and institutional credibility indicators.

NGOs — Corporate Partnerships

ESG communication positioning, reputational return optimisation, impact measurability framing for corporate audiences and sustainability reporting contexts.

Brands

Consumer segment profiling anchored to real demographic data, purchase propensity analysis, premium vs. accessible positioning, rational vs. emotional framing optimisation.

Data & Compliance

Privacy by design.
Zero data collection.

PoliSim's demographic profiling uses exclusively official public datasets (ISTAT, Ministry of Interior). No private citizen data is collected, stored, or processed.

The Data Shield tool (polisim.dev/anonimizza.html) allows institutions to anonymise proprietary datasets before upload. Processing occurs entirely client-side — zero server contact. Compliant with GDPR Art. 4.5, 89, and 25 (data minimisation, archiving for research, privacy by design).

PoliSim does not use data for advertising, does not build citizen profiles, and does not enable micro-targeting of individuals.

Live Case Study

Q-Italia — the
experiment in action.

PoliSim was developed by the founding team of Q-Italia, an Italian political movement built on 14 constitutional principles. Q-Italia uses PoliSim as its live communication optimisation tool — making it the platform's primary case study.

This dual structure — a normative framework (Q-Italia) and an analytical engine (PoliSim) — is designed to demonstrate that principled, data-grounded political communication is technically feasible and operationally deployable.
